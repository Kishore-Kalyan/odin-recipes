Jalebi

Description

Jalebi is a popular sweet in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh, and is often served during festivals and special occasions. It is a deep-fried sweet made by fermenting a mixture of all-purpose flour and yogurt, then frying it until it becomes crisp and golden. The fried jalebis are then soaked in a sugar syrup, which gives them their signature sweet and tangy flavor.

To make Jalebi, you will need all-purpose flour, cornflour, yogurt, baking powder, saffron, and sugar. First, mix together the all-purpose flour, cornflour, baking powder, and yogurt to make a thick batter. Cover the batter and let it ferment for at least 12 hours.

Next, prepare the sugar syrup by heating sugar and water in a pot until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add cardamom, saffron, and rose water for flavor. Meanwhile, heat oil in a frying pan or kadhai.

Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle or a piping bag with a small round nozzle. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a spiral shape, starting from the center and moving outward. Fry the jalebis until they turn crisp and golden brown.

Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried jalebis from the oil and transfer them to the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for a few minutes, then remove and serve hot or at room temperature. Enjoy the sweet, tangy, and crispy goodness of Jalebi!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of saffron
  • Oil, for frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few drops of rose water

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, gram flour, baking powder, and turmeric powder. Mix well.
  2. Add the plain yogurt and water to the dry ingredients, and mix until a smooth batter forms.
  3. Add the rose water to the batter and mix well. Let the batter rest for 10-15 minutes.
  4. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and saffron (if using) for the sugar syrup. Bring the mixture to a boil and let it simmer until it thickens and reaches a one-string consistency.
  5. Add the cardamom powder to the syrup and turn off the heat. Keep the syrup warm.
  6. Heat oil or ghee in a frying pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  7. Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle or a piping bag with a small nozzle.
  8. Once the oil is hot, carefully squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a spiral or circular motion to form jalebi.
  9. Fry the jalebi until they turn golden brown on both sides, flipping them occasionally.
  10. Remove the jalebi from the oil with a slotted spoon and let excess oil drain on a kitchen towel.
  11. Immediately dip the jalebi in the warm sugar syrup, coating them well. Let the jalebi soak in the syrup for 1-2 minutes.
  12. Serve the jalebi warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped pistachios or almonds (optional).
