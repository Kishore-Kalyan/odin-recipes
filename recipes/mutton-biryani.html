Mutton Biryani

Description

Mutton biryani is a flavorful Indian rice dish that combines tender pieces of mutton (goat meat) marinated in a blend of spices with fragrant basmati rice. This aromatic dish features a medley of spices, including cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon, that infuse the meat and rice with rich flavors. Sautéed onions, ginger, and garlic create a savory base, while fresh mint and cilantro add a burst of freshness. Mutton biryani is often garnished with fried onions and served with raita for a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Basmati rice
  • 500 grams of mutton (goat meat), cut into pieces
  • 1 cup of yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup of cooking oil or ghee
  • Whole spices: 2-3 bay leaves, 4-5 green cardamom pods, 4-5 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1/2 cup of fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon of red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon of biryani masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups of water
  • Fried onions (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Wash and soak 2 cups of Basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Marinate 500 grams of mutton pieces with 1 cup of yogurt, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 tablespoon of red chili powder, 1 tablespoon of biryani masala powder, and salt to taste. Keep it refrigerated for at least 30 minutes.
  3. In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, heat 1/2 cup of cooking oil or ghee over medium heat. Add 2-3 bay leaves, 4-5 green cardamom pods, 4-5 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, and 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until they release their aroma.
  4. Add 2 large thinly sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnish.
  5. Add 4 cloves of minced garlic, 1-inch piece of grated ginger, and 2-3 chopped green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw aroma disappears.
  6. Add the marinated mutton to the pan and cook until it's browned on all sides. Stir occasionally.
  7. Add 1/2 cup of fresh mint leaves and 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves. Mix well.
  8. Layer the soaked and drained rice over the mutton mixture in the pan.
  9. Pour 2 cups of water evenly over the rice.
  10. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low. Cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is cooked, and the mutton is tender. You can also place a heavy object on the lid to seal it further.
  11. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing the rice and mutton layers.
  12. Garnish the mutton biryani with the reserved fried onions and serve hot with lemon wedges.
