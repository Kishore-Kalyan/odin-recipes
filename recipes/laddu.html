Laddu

Description

Laddu, also known as laddoo, is a popular Indian sweet that is commonly made during festivals and celebrations. Laddus are small, round balls made of various ingredients such as flour, semolina, coconut, nuts, and sugar. The recipe for laddu can be customized based on individual preferences and taste.

To make laddu, you will need flour, ghee (clarified butter), sugar, cardamom powder, and water. You can also add other ingredients like nuts, coconut, and raisins for added flavor and texture. The process of making laddu is simple and can be done in a few easy steps.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup flour (besan or gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons water
  • Nuts or coconut (optional)

Steps

  1. In a frying pan, heat ghee on low heat until it melts. Add flour and mix well.
  2. Keep stirring the mixture on low heat for around 10-15 minutes until the flour turns golden brown and releases a nutty aroma. Be careful not to burn the flour.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool down for a few minutes.
  4. Add sugar and cardamom powder to the mixture and mix well. You can also add nuts or coconut at this stage.
  5. Add a tablespoon of water at a time and mix until the mixture becomes sticky and can be formed into balls. The amount of water needed may vary depending on the consistency of the mixture.
  6. Take a small portion of the mixture and roll it between your palms to form a ball. Repeat the process with the remaining mixture.
  7. Place the laddus on a plate and let them cool down to room temperature. You can also refrigerate them for a few minutes to help them set.
  8. Serve laddu as a dessert or snack, garnished with chopped nuts or saffron strands (optional).
